Objective

Upon completion of this course, the student will be able to:

  • Apply computer information systems principles and practices in real-world settings.
  • Develop interpersonal and professional skills appropriate for work settings.
  • Demonstrate effective verbal and written communication skills.
  • Apply critical thinking to analyze and solve real world problems.
  • Assess the organizational structure, behaviors and functions within the CIS profession.

Stratford University courses are delivered in three formats: hybrid education, distance education, and externships/clinicals. Hybrid education courses are comprised of face-to-face lecture and/or lab and threaded discussion contact hours. Distance education courses consist of online lecture and/or lab and threaded discussion contact hours. Threaded discussion contact hours are dedicated to student-to-student, student-to-faculty, and student-to-content interaction to demonstrate critical thinking and are always delivered online via the Learning Management System (LMS), Moodle. Threaded discussion contact hours take a minimum of one hour per week and are not homework assignments. Ten hours of threaded discussion contact hours are equivalent to one credit hour. Externship/clinical courses take place outside the classroom and do not require threaded discussion contact hours.

Attendance Policy

Stratford University requires all faculty members take attendance each class period and to record it accurately in the self-service portal. This data is available for verification of attendance by the appropriate governmental agencies and educational accrediting organizations.


Students are expected to attend and be on time for all regularly scheduled campus classes. Online students must demonstrate weekly attendance by submitting academic assignments, completing quizzes or exams, or participating in posted online academic discussions. Logging into an online course without active participation does not constitute attendance.


A student who has been absent due to mitigating circumstances should contact his/her designated department representative for additional time to complete coursework. Mitigating circumstances may include serious illness of the student or immediate family for whom the student is the primary caregiver, death of an immediate family member, military deployment, or unforeseen travel or relocation due to employment.


Students are responsible for any material missed due to tardiness. A student who is late to class may be marked absent at the faculty memberâs discretion. Excessive tardiness makes it difficult for a student to meet the academic objectives of a course.

Honor Code

The Honor Code is a formal process governing student conduct at Stratford University. It governs conduct directly related to academic life of the University and is in effect during all phases of a studentâs academic career. The policy is applicable to any academically related experience involving University students whether occurring on-campus, in a distance learning situation, or at host institutions or sites. All alleged violations of the policy are resolved in accordance with this policy and under the direct authority of a Stratford University faculty member, Campus Dean, and Campus Director.


Breaches to this policy can include, but are not limited to, the following examples:

Cheating: The use or attempted use of unauthorized materials, information, or study aids in any academic exercise is considered cheating.

Plagiarism: Representing the work of another as oneâs own in any academic exercise is considered plagiarism.

Falsification: The invention or alteration of information or citation in an academic exercise is considered falsification. This includes knowingly reporting data, research, or reports as different from what actually occurred; falsely reporting attendance or participation in class, practicum, internship, or other types of field work experience; or submission of falsified excuses for tardiness or absences in such experiences.


When a student is found responsible for a first violation, the faculty member makes the final decision about a grade-related sanction. Additional sanctions, including dismissal from the University, may be recommended by the faculty member, but must be assigned by the Campus Dean and Campus Director. Any second violation, proven or admitted, results in failure of the course and may include dismissal from the University. All recommendations for dismissal by faculty after a second offense must be reviewed and approved by the Campus Dean and Campus Director. The full Honor Code Policy can be found in the University catalog.

TurnItIn Policy

The class instructor may use âTurnitinâ, which is a plagiarism detection software, to evaluate the effectiveness of a studentâs writing skills and a studentâs research skills (including proper citations and use of sources/references). In order to fairly measure these attributes in a student, it is important to validate that each student in this class has submitted an original work. The advantage of using Turnitin is that it helps in identifying if a student plagiarized their work and also generates a report indicating the level of plagiarism. At Stratford University, Turnitin is used as a tool to assist students in the learning process, not as a penalty tool.

When papers are submitted to Turnitin, the service will retain a copy of the submitted work in the Turnitin database for the sole purpose of detecting plagiarism in future submitted works. Students retain copyright on their original course work. The use of Turnitin is subject to the Terms of Use agreement posted on the Turnitin.com website.
